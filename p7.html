About Rohit Sharma

Rohit Gurunath Sharma is an Indian international cricketer and the former captain of the India national cricket team in all formats of the game. He is a right-handed top-order batter. He represents Mumbai in domestic cricket and Mumbai Indians in the Indian Premier League.

Early Life

Born on April 30, 1987, in Nagpur, Maharashtra, Rohit Sharma was raised in Borivali by his grandparents and uncles due to his parents' financial constraints. Raised in a modest Marathi-Telugu household, his talent was spotted by coach Dinesh Lad, who managed his school scholarship to provide better training, transforming him from an off-spinner into a top-order batsman.

International Career

Rohit Sharma is a legendary Indian opening batsman and captain, known as "Hitman" for his explosive batting and record-breaking 6s. Since his 2007 debut, he has redefined limited-overs cricket with three ODI double-centuries and led India to the 2023 ODI World Cup final and T20 World Cup success, solidifying his status as a modern great. International Career Highlights ODI Career (2007–Present): Widely regarded as one of the best ODI openers, he has scored over 11,500+ runs and holds records for the most double centuries (3), including the highest individual score of 264. Test Career (2013–Present): Successfully transitioned to opening in 2019, averaging over 58.48 during his peak, with 12+ centuries. T20I Career (2007–2024): A key player in India’s 2007 T20 World Cup win, he has scored 4,200+ runs and multiple centuries in the format. Captaincy: Led India to the 2023 World Cup final, 2023 WTC Final, and won the 2024 T20 World Cup. Key Records & Stats Most ODI Double Centuries: 3 (264, 209, 208*). Most T20I Centuries: 5. Highest ODI Score: 264 vs Sri Lanka (2014). Fastest T20I Century: Joint-fastest (35 balls). He has played over 60 Tests, 280 ODIs, and 150+ T20Is, cementing his legacy as one of the most destructive and reliable openers in cricket history.

Achievements

  • Holding the world record for the highest individual ODI score (264)
  • As a prolific captain, he led India to the 2024 T20 World Cup
  • Secured five IPL titles with Mumbai Indians
  • He is also the record-holder for the most international sixes.
